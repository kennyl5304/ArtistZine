collaboration

does not

always mean

compromising

your vision

stargazing down a black hole

a fear many artists have is the idea of collaboration. we take pride in our work being created entirely on our own and are frightened of the potential for our vision to be lost in the pursuit of assistance with the creation process.

it may seem appealing to have a project whether its a song or a game created all by yourself, but it also invites the risk of suffering a steep burnout. the precense of other voices can allow for new perespectives to be merged into the final product.

collaboration is the synthesis of perespectives

for example, when developing a game, one cannot bear the burden of coding, creating visuals, audio, and writing the story all on their own without getting overwhelmed quickly. with a team of collaborators each tackling their area of expertise under the direction of a singular creative director, a project can come together quickly without losing the underlying artistic statement the creator intended to make.
