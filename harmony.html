not every imperfect chord is imperfect

in music, just because a chord is slightly off, doesn't mean it'll sound bad. composers will often experiment with arranging chords to be slightly offtonal and play around with the harmonic structure to evoke different feelings.

this idea can be applied to many different creative practices. sometimes, the mistakes can not only be a wonderfully endearing accident, but a jumping on point to make something out of the ordinary.

we've all heard of the saying "so bad it's good", but it's in a more positive light in this context. a mistake can allow us to also change our entire approach and way of thinking, potentially resulting in a more unique outcome.
