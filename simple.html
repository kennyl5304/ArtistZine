Let the existence of Simple Plan remind you to keep things simple

you don't get to greatness right out of the gate, you need to build towards it. the best way to do that is to start off simple and build the foundations first. this approach also helps to reduce the chance of getting overwhelmed.

when coding, you're aiming to simply having a working product. while coding creativily is a different entity altogether, the sentiment still applies that a working product is the ultimate endgoal. once a good framework has been established, then the time is right to start incrementally adding in features.

to use another analogy, complex jazz chords can't exist without the base chord triad underneath it. you can't make a Dmaj7sus2 chord without having a simple D major chord to begin with. moving even further back, creating a simple melody is ideal before reverse engineering a chord progression to fit it.
